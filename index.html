Read more about the design process I follow, from draft ideas to final product.

  • Research

    “Knowing your users” is the most essential asset when crafting digital experiences. Personas, empathy maps, user journeys and flows are my favorite methods to define and analyze user expectations.

    Brainstorming, mood boards, style explorations and checking up on the status of the competition are also important at this stage.

    Bits of inspiration can be found anywhere, therefore I tend to keep my eyes open in all directions.

  • Wireframes

    “Grab a pencil and draw stuff”. This my usual starting point when it comes to UI. It also enables me to get quick feedback and iterate fast, in order to lay the groundwork.

    Sometimes, depending on the needs and philosophy of the stakeholders, I might create more polished wireframes with Sketch or Illustrator.

  • Prototypes

    Prototypes are ideal for showcasing a richer visual output while letting everyone interact with the interface and get a better feel of the experience.

    Among many notable tools, I've picked InVision for app-wide flows and Principle for micro-interactions.

  • Mockups

    This is the pixel-perfect part of the process in all its glory. Colors, typography, grids, icons and images convey the final look and feel in detail.

    Quality mockups in terms of fidelity and consistency are the solid foundations of a successful development phase.

  • User Testing

    There is no silver bullet when building complex interfaces. Working with data and studying analytics help me extract useful insights and strike a balance between assumptions and facts.

    Usability tests, interviews, recordings, heatmaps, conversion funnels and retention analytics can all be used to reveal any pain points and misconceptions.

    I believe the key to creating delightful experiences lies in understanding and evaluating the user's voice.
