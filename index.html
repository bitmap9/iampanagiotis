Workable Mobile

Over the past few years we've been building great stuff at Workable. We've put a lot of effort to create a great mobile experience for those who hire on the go.

My responsibility was to design the apps for three platforms: iOS, Android and Mobile Web. As challenging as it sounds, working on this product with an awesome team, was is an amazing journey.

Research

"Knowing your users" is the most essential asset when crafting digital experiences. Personas, journeys and flows are my favorite methods to define and analyze (the) users' expectations.

Brainstorming, mood boards, style explorations and checking the competition status are also important at this stage.

Bits of inspiration can be found anywhere, therefore I tend to keep my eyes open at every direction.

Wireframes

Grab a pencil and draw stuff. This my usual starting point when it comes to UI. It also enables me to get quick feedback and iterate fast, in order to lay the groundwork.

Sometimes, depending on the stakeholders group, I create more polished wireframes with Sketch or Illustrator.

Prototypes

Presenting static screens to your peers and let them imagine how it works, is not the way to go. Prototypes are ideal for showcasing a richer visual output while letting everyone involved get a better feel of the experience.

Among many notable tools, I've picked InVision for app-wide flows and Principle for micro-interactions.

Mockups

This is the pixel-perfect part in all its glory. Colors, typography, grids, icons and images convey the final look and feel in detail.

Quality mockups in terms of fidelity and consistency are the solid foundations of the development phase.

User Testing

There is no silver bullet when building complex interfaces. Working with data and studying analytics help(s) me extract useful insights and balance between assumptions and facts.

Usability tests, interviews, recordings, heatmaps, conversion funnels and retention analytics will reveal any pain points and misconceptions.

Understating and evaluating the users' voice is the key for creating delightful experiences.
